# Strategic Direction

## Objective 1 — International Expansion

PARTTEAM & OEMKIOSKS aims to grow its international revenue share by expanding
its active presence in markets where it already has traction — specifically Spain,
France, the United Kingdom, and the Middle East — while opening new distributor
partnerships in Central and Eastern Europe.

The company has produced units for 35+ countries but direct commercial presence
remains concentrated in Portugal. The strategic priority is to convert existing
international deployments into structured partner relationships and recurring
revenue, rather than one-off export sales.

### Signal Relevance — International Expansion

- **HIGH** — competitor wins a distribution or partnership deal in a target market;
  new EU-funded digital infrastructure programmes announced in target regions;
  a target market government launches a smart city or transport digitalisation tender
- **MEDIUM** — competitor expands manufacturing capacity in Europe; industry
  reports signal accelerating kiosk adoption in a target region
- **LOW** — competitor activity in markets outside Europe and the Middle East;
  general globalisation news unrelated to kiosk or DOOH infrastructure

## Objective 2 — QSR and Food Service Market Growth

PARTTEAM & OEMKIOSKS aims to grow its presence in the Quick Service Restaurant
and food service vertical across Europe, targeting mid-size restaurant chains
(20–200 locations) that are actively replacing counter-only ordering with
self-service kiosk systems.

The company has proven deployments in this vertical (H3, Burger King Greece,
Nova Peixaria) and a dedicated software product (YSnack / YCanteen). The
strategic priority is to convert this into a repeatable, scalable sales motion
with standardised hardware and software bundles that reduce deployment time
and lower the barrier to entry for mid-size chains.

### Signal Relevance — QSR and Food Service Market Growth

- **HIGH** — competitor wins a QSR chain rollout in Europe; major QSR chain
  announces self-ordering kiosk programme; new EU accessibility or labour
  regulations accelerate self-service adoption in food service
- **MEDIUM** — competitor launches new QSR-specific kiosk hardware or software;
  payment technology changes affect kiosk ordering flows (e.g. new contactless
  or biometric payment standards)
- **LOW** — QSR news unrelated to self-service technology; competitor activity
  in QSR markets outside Europe

## Objective 3 — Smart Cities and Public Procurement

PARTTEAM & OEMKIOSKS aims to increase its win rate on EU-funded smart city
and public infrastructure tenders, targeting municipal digital transformation
programmes, citizen services kiosks, passenger information systems, and
urban DOOH deployments.

The company has existing deployments with multiple Portuguese municipalities
and transport operators, and has been referenced in the Smart Cities Yearbook
2024 edition. The strategic priority is to leverage these reference deployments
to compete for larger EU-funded tenders beyond Portugal, using the TED
(Tenders Electronic Daily) procurement platform as the primary sourcing channel.

### Signal Relevance — Smart Cities and Public Procurement

- **HIGH** — new EU smart city or digital infrastructure tenders published on
  TED matching kiosk, MUPI, passenger information, or citizen services
  categories; competitor wins or loses a major public sector contract in
  a target market; new EU cohesion fund programmes announced for digital
  public infrastructure
- **MEDIUM** — smart city policy announcements at national or EU level that
  signal future procurement activity; competitor forms a partnership with
  a systems integrator active in public sector procurement
- **LOW** — smart city news unrelated to physical digital infrastructure;
  public procurement activity in non-EU markets

## Objective 4 — Software and Recurring Revenue Growth

PARTTEAM & OEMKIOSKS aims to grow the share of recurring revenue from
software licences, SaaS subscriptions, and maintenance contracts relative
to one-off hardware sales. The company has a portfolio of proprietary
software products (YSnack, YPortal, YSCO Market, The Brain) that are
currently underleveraged as standalone revenue streams.

The strategic priority is to evolve from a hardware manufacturer with
bundled software into a hardware-plus-software company where software
and services generate predictable, margin-accretive recurring revenue
independent of hardware sales cycles.

### Signal Relevance — Software and Recurring Revenue Growth

- **HIGH** — competitor launches or acquires a SaaS platform for kiosk
  management or digital signage; major kiosk software platform announces
  European expansion; new industry standard for remote kiosk monitoring
  or content management emerges
- **MEDIUM** — competitor announces a subscription-based pricing model
  for hardware or software; cloud-based CMS platforms gain significant
  traction in the DOOH or self-service market
- **LOW** — software news unrelated to kiosk management, digital signage,
  or self-service operations; competitor software activity in non-target
  verticals

## Objective 5 — Product Innovation and Design Leadership

PARTTEAM & OEMKIOSKS aims to maintain and strengthen its position as a
design-led hardware manufacturer, using product design awards and
innovation credentials as a commercial differentiator in competitive
tender processes and enterprise sales.

The company has won the European Product Design Awards four times
(2020, 2021, 2023, 2025) and holds the COTEC Innovative Statute. The
strategic priority is to continue investing in industrial design and
emerging hardware technologies — including touchless interaction,
AI-assisted interfaces, IoT connectivity, and sustainable materials —
to ensure the product catalogue remains competitive against both European
and Asian manufacturers.

### Signal Relevance — Product Innovation and Design Leadership

- **HIGH** — competitor wins a major design or innovation award in the
  kiosk or DOOH category; new touchless, AI, or IoT hardware capabilities
  emerge that could redefine customer expectations for self-service kiosks;
  a low-cost Asian manufacturer enters the European market with a
  design-competitive product
- **MEDIUM** — new display technologies (e-paper, transparent displays,
  high-brightness outdoor screens) become commercially viable for kiosk
  deployment; competitor announces a significant product refresh or new
  hardware line
- **LOW** — design awards in unrelated product categories; hardware
  innovation news unrelated to kiosk, DOOH, or interactive systems

## Objective 6 — OEM/ODM Partner Network Growth

PARTTEAM & OEMKIOSKS aims to grow its OEM/ODM manufacturing business
by acquiring new international partners and distributors who resell or
rebrand PARTTEAM hardware in their local markets. This channel requires
no direct commercial presence in the target market and generates
high-volume, long-term manufacturing contracts with predictable demand.

The company already operates a structured partner programme with a
dedicated partner area, NDA process, and referral programme. The
strategic priority is to convert inbound international interest into
formal distribution agreements, particularly in markets where the company
has had one-off deployments but no structured partner relationship —
including Central and Eastern Europe, the Middle East, and North Africa.

### Signal Relevance — OEM/ODM Partner Network Growth

- **HIGH** — competitor signs a new distribution or OEM agreement in a
  target region; a systems integrator or technology reseller in a target
  market publicly announces a search for a kiosk hardware manufacturing
  partner; competitor loses a major distribution partner
- **MEDIUM** — industry consolidation among European kiosk distributors;
  a target market government introduces incentives for locally-assembled
  digital infrastructure that could favour an OEM partnership model
- **LOW** — OEM/ODM news in non-target markets; manufacturing partnerships
  unrelated to kiosk or digital signage hardware
