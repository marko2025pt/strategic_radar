# Technology Watchlist

## Touchless and Gesture-Based Interaction

Touchless kiosk interfaces using hand gesture recognition, proximity sensors,
and computer vision are emerging as an alternative to physical touchscreens.
Driven initially by post-COVID hygiene concerns, the technology is maturing
into a genuine UX differentiator for high-traffic public environments such
as airports, transport hubs, and hospitals. Key enabling technologies include
Intel RealSense depth sensors, Leap Motion controllers, and custom proximity
detection modules integrated directly into kiosk hardware.

Relevance: direct threat and opportunity for PARTTEAM hardware design.
A competitor that ships a credible touchless kiosk line before PARTTEAM
could redefine buyer expectations in the public sector vertical, particularly
in healthcare and transport where hygiene requirements are strictest.
PARTTEAM has publicly signalled interest in touchless interaction — hand
motion and card swipe without screen contact — making this a live product
development priority. Any competitor touchless launch or major public sector
deployment of touchless kiosk technology warrants immediate attention.

## AI-Driven Personalisation and Upsell at the Kiosk

Machine learning models embedded in kiosk software are being used to
personalise menu recommendations, dynamically reorder product displays,
and optimise upsell prompts based on time of day, weather, purchase
history, and real-time inventory. Acrelec is already deploying this
capability at scale in QSR environments, and research shows AI-driven
upsell can increase average order values by up to 30% in food service
settings.

Relevance: direct threat to PARTTEAM's YSnack and QSR kiosk software
competitiveness. If mid-size QSR chains begin expecting AI-driven upsell
as a standard feature, PARTTEAM's software stack will need to match this
capability to remain competitive in the food service vertical. Also
relevant to the retail self-checkout vertical where personalisation
at the point of sale is an emerging expectation. Any competitor software
update, partnership with an AI recommendation engine provider, or QSR
chain announcement about AI-driven ordering is strategically relevant.

## Programmatic DOOH and DSP Integration

Programmatic buying of Digital Out-of-Home advertising inventory is
growing rapidly in Europe, driven by DSP platforms such as Broadsign
Reach, Hivestack, Vistar Media, and Place Exchange. Media buyers can
now purchase DOOH ad slots in real time alongside digital display and
video inventory, using audience data and contextual triggers such as
weather, time, and footfall.

Relevance: direct opportunity for PARTTEAM. The company operates a
network of deployed DOOH screens across municipalities, transport hubs,
and public spaces. Integrating a programmatic advertising layer into
the CMS platform would unlock advertising revenue from existing
deployments without additional hardware investment. Broadsign is the
dominant software layer in European DOOH and represents both a
potential integration partner and a competitive threat to PARTTEAM's
own CMS platform.

## Computer Vision and Audience Measurement

Computer vision systems mounted on or near DOOH displays and kiosks
are being used to measure audience demographics, dwell time, attention
metrics, and emotional response in real time. This data is used to
optimise content scheduling, prove advertising ROI to media buyers,
and feed programmatic buying algorithms. Key players include Quividi,
Admobilize, and Alfi.

Relevance: directly relevant to PARTTEAM's DOOH and digital billboard
product line. Advertisers and municipalities increasingly expect
audience measurement as a built-in capability rather than an add-on.
A competitor that ships displays with embedded audience analytics will
have a stronger value proposition in programmatic DOOH tenders.
Also relevant to the QSR vertical where computer vision is being used
for order accuracy verification and customer flow optimisation at
the kiosk — a capability Acrelec has already deployed in partnership
with XXII.

## IoT Connectivity and Predictive Maintenance

IoT-enabled kiosks and displays transmit real-time operational data —
temperature, screen brightness, component health, network latency,
and transaction errors — to cloud management platforms. Machine learning
models applied to this data can predict hardware failures before they
occur, enabling preventive maintenance that reduces unplanned downtime
and field service costs.

Relevance: directly relevant to PARTTEAM's The Brain remote management
platform and maintenance service contracts. Clients in public sector
and transport verticals have strict uptime requirements and long-term
service agreements where unplanned downtime has contractual penalties.
A competitor that offers AI-driven predictive maintenance as a standard
platform feature will have a stronger argument in public procurement
tenders where SLA guarantees are a key evaluation criterion. This
technology also supports PARTTEAM's ability to reduce field service
costs and improve margins on maintenance contracts.

## Edge Computing for Kiosks and Digital Signage

Edge computing deployments push processing power directly into kiosk
and display hardware, enabling real-time data processing, AI inference,
and content personalisation without reliance on cloud connectivity.
This is particularly relevant for transport and municipal deployments
where network reliability cannot be guaranteed. Key enablers include
Intel NUC, NVIDIA Jetson, and purpose-built edge AI modules.

Relevance: directly relevant to PARTTEAM's transport and smart city
vertical where kiosks and passenger information displays must operate
reliably in environments with intermittent or low-bandwidth connectivity.
Edge computing also enables on-device AI capabilities — touchless
interaction, audience measurement, personalisation — without the
latency and data privacy concerns of cloud-dependent architectures.
GDPR compliance is a key consideration for EU public sector clients,
and on-device processing reduces the data residency risk associated
with sending biometric or behavioural data to external cloud platforms.

## Accessibility and Inclusive Design Standards

The European Accessibility Act (EAA) came into force in June 2025,
requiring self-service terminals — including kiosks, ticketing machines,
and payment terminals — to meet strict accessibility standards for
users with visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive impairments. This
includes requirements for screen reader compatibility, audio output,
adjustable display height, tactile controls, and simplified interfaces.

Relevance: direct regulatory driver for PARTTEAM's entire hardware
portfolio. Public sector and transport clients — already the primary
buyers of PARTTEAM products — will require EAA-compliant hardware in
all new procurement from June 2025 onwards. This is simultaneously
a threat (existing hardware may require redesign) and an opportunity
(competitors whose products are not EAA-compliant will be disqualified
from EU public tenders). PARTTEAM's existing focus on compliance-ready
design and public sector procurement positions it well to capitalise
on this regulatory shift if it moves faster than competitors.
