Your market doesn't
wait for your next meeting.
The Situation
Markets move faster than your intelligence cycle.
Competitors announce acquisitions on Tuesday. Tenders close on Thursday. A city council approves a €2M smart mobility budget — and the positioning window opens and closes before anyone on your team even sees the signal.

Good strategic intelligence exists. It's just scattered across dozens of sources, buried in noise, and nobody has time to read it all.
The Reality
Your team is making decisions on incomplete information.
Not because the information doesn't exist — but because collecting it, filtering it, and connecting it to your strategy takes more hours than anyone has available. The result is reactive decisions, missed tender windows, and a competitor advantage that compounds quietly over time.
If nothing changes
You keep winning on instinct — until you don't.
Manual scanning stays a part-time job for someone already at capacity. Competitor moves get noticed weeks late. Tenders appear on your radar after the positioning window has closed. The market shifts — and the first signal you get is a lost deal.
The Ideal
Every Monday morning, leadership already knows what matters.
Competitor moves ranked by strategic impact — not by publish date. Live tenders surfaced before the deadline pressure starts. Pre-tender signals caught months early, when positioning is still possible.

One page. Ranked by impact. With a recommended action. Delivered before the week begins — without anyone having to look for it.
